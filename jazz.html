Timpots Forever — Jazz

July 4, 1930 — March 22, 2026

Mercy Jeyaraja Rao — “Jazz”

For many who lived in Visakhapatnam and Andhra Pradesh since the 1960s, the name Jeyaraja Rao brings vivid memories of a great teacher, a leader and pioneer in the field of education. Born in Visakhapatnam, Mercy Jeyaraja Rao joined Timpany School in 1954 and served for forty-eight years, as a teacher, principal and director of the schools she helped build, before retiring in 2002. Under her hand the school grew from a single small room with ten students into a comprehensive institution of three buildings serving several thousand students annually. In 1964 she led its move to the CBM Compound campus and its transformation into a high school. Beyond Timpany she is known for her work in the Baptist World Alliance and the First Baptist Church in Knoxville, Tennessee.

To her students she was, in equal measure, a terror and a refuge. Tall and unhurried, she walked the corridors with a poise no one could overshadow: bold-coloured sarees, a single flower tucked in her bun, a voice that “drilled through mind, heart and soul.” 7:55 a.m. was a line not to be crossed; handwriting was inspected; haircuts were announced over the intercom. She introduced softball when few Indian schools did, set up Timpany's first computer lab, and on the first day of each academic year retold the story of the Little Blue Engine that thought it could — a story many of her students say they still live by.

What softened the discipline was the constancy of her care. Decades after graduation she wrote handwritten letters to alumni in crisis, met them at airports for surgery, remembered the anniversaries of their losses, and in later years apologized for the strappings and the fears she had caused. However her students, “my precious children” as she addressed them, remember the values of discipline and hard work that she instilled with gratitude. Today “Timpots” live across the globe and serve their communities in several capacities, as leaders, volunteers and good citizens, living up to the principles she taught. The thousands molded are recognisable, as one of them put it, by their bearing, their handwriting, and their dedication to professionalism and service in every corner of the world.

Her legacy will live for a long time and shape millions of lives to come.

She was not merely an administrator; she was an institution in herself.

The passing of Mercy Jeyaraja Rao, former Principal of Timpany School, Visakhapatnam, marks the end of an era defined by dedication, compassion, and unwavering commitment to education.

Mercy Jeyaraja Rao was not merely an administrator; she was an institution in herself. As the guiding force behind Timpany School, she shaped generations of students with a rare blend of discipline and empathy. Her leadership stood as a model of integrity — firm in standards, yet deeply humane in approach. She believed that education was not confined to textbooks but extended to character, values, and responsibility toward society.

Under her stewardship, Timpany School grew not only in academic excellence but also in moral stature. She encouraged students to think critically, act ethically, and strive for excellence in every sphere of life. Many who passed through the corridors during her tenure went on to become responsible citizens, carrying forward the imprint of her teachings.

Her colleagues remember her as a visionary leader who upheld the highest standards of professionalism while fostering a spirit of unity and purpose. She inspired teachers to go beyond conventional methods, nurturing young minds with patience and dedication.

Beyond her professional achievements, she was known for her humility, kindness, and quiet strength. She touched countless lives — not through grand gestures, but through consistent care, guidance, and encouragement.

Her legacy lives on in every student she mentored, every teacher she inspired, and every life she shaped. Though she is no longer with us, her values and contributions will continue to illuminate the path for future generations.

May her soul rest in eternal peace.

She noticed the small things, encouraged without fuss, and set a tone that stayed with the school.

The late Ms Jeyaraja Rao led with quiet strength and clarity. She was firm but fair, and deeply invested in the growth of her students — not just academically, but in how they carried themselves. She noticed the small things, encouraged without fuss, and set a tone that stayed with the school.

Her belief that confidence comes with practice showed in the morning assemblies. We were given topics in advance, but standing up to speak in front of the entire school was never easy. There were nerves, pauses, and uncertainty. Yet, over time, those moments helped us find our voice and speak with greater ease.

She also ensured that sports had a meaningful place in school life. Under her leadership, softball was introduced — likely among the first schools in India to do so. 👏 It added something new to the rhythm of school, and quickly became a distinctive part of it.

What she created was not loud or showy, but it was lasting — a school where students grew in confidence, step by step, under her steady guidance and encouragement.

I was privileged to meet her once again in Bangalore and will always cherish my memories of this angel who walked this earth. 🙏

A batch of 24 students started their journey in January 1966 and finished in December 1974.

The teachers were very good and caring. And Ms Paul, the office clerk.

Ms Jeyaraja Rao's classes on English Literature were the ultimate.

We passed out in December 1974, totally 22 of us, to pursue our lives. Walked into Timpany school at the age of 6. Now we are 68. But our minds are still fresh with the memories of our good old school, and we still proudly call ourselves "Timpots."
