How It Began

Out of a memorial, a movement.

When Jazz passed on March 22, 2026, Timpots came home. In Vizag, Meenakshi Anantram and other alumni organized a moving memorial on Timpany's own grounds — a place she had walked for forty-eight years. In late March, several Timpots travelled to Dallas for her funeral. The rest of us tuned in from wherever in the world we happened to be.

What followed was a flood. A WhatsApp group grew past 600 Timpots within weeks. Hundreds of tributes were written. Old photographs began surfacing — class pictures from the 60s, 70s, 80s, faces and names not seen in decades. Former teachers joined the chat. Songs were hummed across time zones.

Out of those weeks of mourning, three alumni groups quietly self-organized — one builds this website, one is launching The Timpany Times, our bi-monthly newsletter, and one organizes memorial events and giving-back campaigns. Regular meetups are being planned. Jazz's work, continued — and only just begun.
