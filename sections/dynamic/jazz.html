Remembering Jazz.

Dr. Mercy Jeyaraja Rao — known to generations of Timpots simply as Jazz — served Timpany School for forty-eight years, from 1954 to 2002, rising from teacher to Headmistress, Principal, Executive Director, and General Director. Born in Visakhapatnam on July 4, 1930, she led the school through its transformation into a high school and its 1964 move to Waltair. Beyond Timpany, she served globally as President of the Asian Baptist Women's Union and in the Baptist World Alliance. She was, as one alumnus put it, "a strict yet God-fearing, loving, amazing woman" — and in her care, thousands of Timpots were shaped into who they became. This is where we gather her stories, letters, photographs, and the lessons she left behind.
