The Jeyaraja Rao Memorial Debate Competition

Giving children the courage to speak — and the conviction to be heard.

Jazz believed that a child who could stand and speak the truth — clearly, fearlessly, in front of a room — could do almost anything else in life. For forty-eight years at Timpany, she watched students find their voices, and she never stopped insisting that public speaking was not a luxury. It was preparation for citizenship.

The Jeyaraja Rao Memorial Debate Competition is our annual tribute to that belief. Each year, students from schools across Andhra Pradesh — many from underserved communities where a second language and a stage are rarely offered together — come to Timpany to argue, to listen, to lose well, and to win graciously. Travel, coaching, and accommodation are provided at no cost to participating schools.

Your support funds student stipends, debate coaches, the travelling library of primers, and the trophy that bears her name.
