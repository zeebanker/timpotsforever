Our Story · Since 1931

A history worth carrying forward.

Timpany School was founded in 1931 under the Canadian Baptist Mission and named for Rev. Americus Vespucius (A.V.) Timpany (1840–1885) — the pioneering Canadian Baptist missionary to India whose work in Kakinada and Ramapatnam shaped the mission's legacy in Andhra. From a single small room with ten students to the landmark institution in Asilmetta we know today, the school has shaped generations of Vizagites. Explore the archive — the founder's story, photographs of the old campus, the 1964 move to Waltair, class photos across the decades, and the voices of alumni who carry Timpany with them across the world.
