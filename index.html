In Loving Memory

Remembering Jazz.

Dr. Mercy Jeyaraja Rao — known to generations of Timpots simply as Jazz — served Timpany School for forty-eight years, from 1954 to 2002, rising from teacher to Headmistress, Principal, Executive Director, and General Director. Born in Visakhapatnam on July 4, 1930, she led the school through its transformation into a high school and its 1964 move to Waltair. Beyond Timpany, she served globally as President of the Asian Baptist Women's Union and in the Baptist World Alliance. She was, as one alumnus put it, "a strict yet God-fearing, loving, amazing woman" — and in her care, thousands of Timpots were shaped into who they became. This is where we gather her stories, letters, photographs, and the lessons she left behind.

Our Story · Since 1931

A history worth carrying forward.

Timpany School was founded in 1931 by Reverend A.E. Timpany of the Canadian Baptist Mission, first in Kakinada and later in Vizag. From a single small room with ten students to the landmark institution in Asilmetta and Waltair we know today, the school has shaped generations of Vizagites. Explore the archive — founding documents, photographs of the old campus, the 1964 move to Waltair, class photos across the decades, and the voices of alumni who carry Timpany with them across the world.

Philanthropy · Featured Cause

The Jeyaraja Rao Memorial Debate Competition

Giving children the courage to speak — and the conviction to be heard.

Jazz believed that a child who could stand and speak the truth — clearly, fearlessly, in front of a room — could do almost anything else in life. For forty-eight years at Timpany, she watched students find their voices, and she never stopped insisting that public speaking was not a luxury. It was preparation for citizenship.

The Jeyaraja Rao Memorial Debate Competition is our annual tribute to that belief. Each year, students from schools across Andhra Pradesh — many from underserved communities where a second language and a stage are rarely offered together — come to Timpany to argue, to listen, to lose well, and to win graciously. Travel, coaching, and accommodation are provided at no cost to participating schools.

Your support funds student stipends, debate coaches, the travelling library of primers, and the trophy that bears her name.
